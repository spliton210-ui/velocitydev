## Selection Criteria (Critical)

### ‚úÖ You'll Consider Equity Partnerships When:

**The Founder:**
- Has domain expertise in the industry
- Demonstrates strong work ethic and commitment
- Has complementary skills (sales, marketing, industry connections)
- Is full-time or going full-time on this
- Has skin in the game (their own time/money invested)
- Communicates clearly and professionally

**The Idea:**
- Solves a real problem (not just "cool idea")
- Has validation (customers expressing interest, letters of intent, waitlist)
- Clear path to revenue (not "we'll figure that out later")
- Reasonable scope for AI-accelerated development
- You understand the market enough to evaluate it
- Not in an industry you're uncomfortable with

**The Business Model:**
- SaaS with recurring revenue potential
- B2B with clear customer acquisition path
- Marketplace with network effects
- Platform with scale potential
- NOT: Low-margin, highly competitive commodity plays

**The Deal:**
- Equity stake is meaningful (10%+ for MVP, 15%+ for full build)
- Clear vesting terms (protects both parties)
- Clean cap table (not 12 advisors with 2% each)
- Founder owns majority (you're partner, not co-founder)
- Exit potential realistic (M&A or growth to profitability)

### ‚ùå You'll Pass When:

- "Just an idea" with no validation
- Founder won't go full-time
- Unclear business model
- Overly complex technical requirements
- Regulated industries (healthcare, finance) without proper expertise
- Founder wants you to do sales/marketing too
- "We just need a developer" attitude (not valuing what you bring)
- Cap table is a mess
- Founder has done this 5 times before (serial idea person)
- Your equity would be <10% for significant work

## Risk Mitigation

### For You:
1. **Minimum cash threshold** ($1K+ even for equity deals)
2. **Vesting schedules** (equity vests over time)
3. **Clear scope limits** (no scope creep on equity deals)
4. **Selection criteria** (be picky, most will fail anyway)
5. **Legal protection** (simple equity agreements, reviewed by lawyer once)
6. **Time caps** (max 12 weeks per equity project)

### For Founders:
1. **Vesting both ways** (you don't just disappear after build)
2. **Clear deliverables** (what they're getting defined upfront)
3. **Support period** (you're there post-launch)
4. **Technical advisory** (ongoing strategic guidance)
5. **Fair equity** (not taking advantage)

---

## Legal Essentials

### You Need:
1. **Simple Equity Agreement Template** (get lawyer to draft once, ~$1K-2K)
2. **Service Agreement** (what you're building)
3. **IP Assignment** (you retain some rights to code patterns, they get exclusive use)
4. **Vesting Schedule Terms** (protect both parties)

### Key Terms to Include:
- Equity percentage
- Vesting schedule (12-24 months typical)
- Role definition (advisor, not employee)
- Time commitment (X hours per month post-launch)
- Exit provisions (what happens if acquired)
- IP ownership (they own the specific product, you keep general code patterns)

**Investment:** $1,500-3,000 for lawyer to create templates you reuse
